Closets, Please, and the Bigger the Better

Sasha Maslov for The New York Times

Talk about customization: Brian Lewis and his daughter Pearl pay a call on the aquarium in the walk-in closet of the master bedroom in their Upper West Side apartment.

Tina Fineberg for The New York Times

In her apartment in Park Slope, Brooklyn, Melissa Bartolini Kearney axed the breakfast nook in favor of an additional closet.

Guess what, no one has enough closet space. In my immediate family 3 of us have large homes 3, 4, and one 10,000 sq. ft. Now for the younger folks a couple 1,500 sq. ft condo's. She was in the process of moving to a larger apartment in order to increase her closet space. If our lives as Americans weren't so empty, we wouldn't need to fill void with more clothes and stuff than anyone could possibly use. The first half of your life you spend accumulating stuff; the second half getting rid of it. I love the Times, but sometimes these in depth articles on the trifling non-problems of the 1% living in Manhattan and Park Slope drive me crazy. Can we please make a bonfire of this form of vanity ? Just a bunch of greedy Americans. I
came across a bag of McDonald's fries with a receipt from three months ago.
What's next- my super sized toilet?
